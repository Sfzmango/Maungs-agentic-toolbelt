# ExampleApp — AGENTS.md

## Overview

ExampleApp is a multi-tenant B2B SaaS helpdesk. Each customer company is an **Organization** (the tenant); its people are **Members** with a role (`owner`, `agent`, `viewer`) who work support **Tickets** (which carry **Comments** and move `open → pending → resolved → closed`). Billing attaches a **Plan** (`free`, `team`, `business`) and issues **Invoices** per billing period. Every record belongs to exactly one Organization, and an Organization's data is invisible to every other Organization.

## The cardinal rule — multi-tenant isolation

Every record is scoped to an Organization. A Member of org A must never read, write, or even **discover** a Ticket, Comment, Invoice, or Member belonging to org B. **Cross-tenant access returns 404, NOT 403** — a 403 leaks the existence of the resource. Read org-owned records only through the current organization's associations (e.g. `current_organization.tickets.find(...)`), never a bare `Model.find(params[:id])`. This is the project's #1 bug class; every org-scoped action needs a cross-tenant-returns-404 test.

## Gotchas

- **Multi-tenant isolation is the #1 bug class** — see the cardinal rule. The common failure is `Model.find(params[:id])` on an org-owned table instead of scoping through `current_organization`.
- **Team invitations (EX-218):** the invitation generates a random token, emails the **raw** value in the accept link, and persists only its **SHA-256 digest** — never store or log the raw token. The token is single-use (consumed on acceptance) and expires after **7 days**. The accept route is the **only unauthenticated surface** (token-guarded, since the invitee has no account yet) and derives the Organization **from the invitation**, never from a request parameter, so a member can only ever be minted in the issuing org. Create / revoke / resend are **owner-only**; only `agent` and `viewer` roles can be granted by invitation.
- **Don't bypass the pre-commit hook** (`--no-verify`); CI runs the full suite regardless.
- **Transactional email is async** (background job), so a mail hiccup never blocks the request; **Resend** is the recovery path. `(needs confirmation — confirm the job/adapter wiring.)`
